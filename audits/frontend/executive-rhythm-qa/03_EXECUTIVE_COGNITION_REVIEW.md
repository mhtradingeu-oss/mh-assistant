# Executive Cognition Review — MH-OS Control Center Frontend

## Executive Conclusion

The current executive rhythm pages show strong functional intent but do not yet deliver the visual and cognitive clarity expected of an AI Business Operating System. The Home page is dense and reads as an internal dashboard, making it difficult for executives to focus on the most important signals and actions. AI Command has a clear conceptual model but suffers from workspace compression and unclear separation between conversation, output, and workflow. Governance is the cleanest, but lower sections lose executive focus and become report-like. All pages need improved hierarchy, calmer surfaces, and more explicit next actions to achieve a launch-ready executive UX.

## Home Review

**1. What is the page trying to help the user decide?**
- The Home page aims to help the user understand overall system health, readiness, blockers, and what to do next at an executive level.

**2. Is the page mission clear within 5 seconds?**
- No. The density of metrics, cards, and operational details makes the mission ambiguous for new or executive users.

**3. Is the next best action clear?**
- Partially. The "Next Best Action" section exists but is visually buried among other signals and lacks strong visual priority.

**4. Is the AI role clear?**
- No. The AI's role is not explicitly surfaced; it is implied through scattered cards and action buttons.

**5. Is the evidence/source/supporting data clear?**
- No. There is a large volume of data, but the provenance and executive relevance of each signal is not obvious.

**6. Is the page showing too much raw data?**
- Yes. The page surfaces too many operational details and metrics without clear executive abstraction or prioritization.

**7. Is the page visually calm or too dense?**
- Too dense. The layout and information density create cognitive overload.

**8. Does it feel like an executive operating surface?**
- Not yet. It feels like an advanced dashboard for operators, not a calm, high-level executive surface.

**9. What should be hidden, collapsed, renamed, or promoted?**
- Collapse or hide secondary metrics and operational details by default.
- Promote the "Next Best Action" and executive summary to the top.
- Rename sections to use executive language ("System Health" → "Executive Readiness").

**10. What is the safest UX improvement for this page?**
- Drastically reduce visible metrics by default. Show only the top 1-2 executive signals and the next best action. Move operational details to collapsible panels.

## AI Command Review

**1. What is the page trying to help the user decide?**
- Which AI specialist or workflow to engage for campaign, content, or operational guidance.

**2. Is the page mission clear within 5 seconds?**
- Partially. The specialist/team model is strong, but the workspace feels compressed and the mission is not visually prioritized.

**3. Is the next best action clear?**
- Partially. The prompt composer and quick actions exist but compete for attention with tabs and tool docks.

**4. Is the AI role clear?**
- Yes, but only after exploring. The specialist/team structure is clear in code but not visually dominant.

**5. Is the evidence/source/supporting data clear?**
- No. Outputs and supporting data are mixed together, making it hard to distinguish guidance from evidence.

**6. Is the page showing too much raw data?**
- Yes. Conversation, output, and workspace areas are compressed and not clearly separated.

**7. Is the page visually calm or too dense?**
- Too dense. The workspace lacks whitespace and clear separation between functional areas.

**8. Does it feel like an executive operating surface?**
- Not yet. It feels like a power-user tool for operators.

**9. What should be hidden, collapsed, renamed, or promoted?**
- Separate conversation, output, and workspace into distinct, collapsible panels.
- Promote the AI specialist/team selection visually.
- Collapse advanced tool docks by default.

**10. What is the safest UX improvement for this page?**
- Visually separate the conversation, output, and workspace areas. Make the AI specialist/team selection persistent and prominent.

## Governance Review

**1. What is the page trying to help the user decide?**
- Which governance/approval/escalation decisions require attention and what the safest next action is.

**2. Is the page mission clear within 5 seconds?**
- Yes. The command center and summary grid are clear and focused.

**3. Is the next best action clear?**
- Mostly. The next action is present but could be more visually prioritized.

**4. Is the AI role clear?**
- Partially. "Prepare only" is shown, but the AI's limits and authority are not always explicit.

**5. Is the evidence/source/supporting data clear?**
- Partially. Evidence and escalation chains are present but sometimes buried in lower sections.

**6. Is the page showing too much raw data?**
- Less than others, but lower sections become report-like and lose executive focus.

**7. Is the page visually calm or too dense?**
- Calmer than others, but still dense in lower sections.

**8. Does it feel like an executive operating surface?**
- Closer than others, but still has report-like areas.

**9. What should be hidden, collapsed, renamed, or promoted?**
- Collapse audit history and lower report sections by default.
- Promote escalation path and authority owner.
- Use more executive language in section headers.

**10. What is the safest UX improvement for this page?**
- Collapse lower report sections and promote the escalation/authority summary at the top.

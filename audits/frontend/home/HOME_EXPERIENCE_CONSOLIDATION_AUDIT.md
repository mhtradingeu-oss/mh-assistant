# HOME EXPERIENCE CONSOLIDATION AUDIT

## 1. Executive Hierarchy Audit
- **Visual Hierarchy:**
  - Executive Health Strip is visually primary and anchors the top of the page.
  - Next Best Action is highly visible and draws immediate attention.
  - Workforce Room and Workflow Chain are prominent but secondary to the above.
  - Escalation Lane is visible but calm, not alarming.
  - Executive Snapshot and AI Guidance are clearly separated.
- **Scan Order:**
  - Executives see Health Strip → Next Best Action → Workflow/Workforce → Escalation → Snapshot/Guidance.
- **Decision Visibility:**
  - Key decisions (health, next action, blockers) are visible above the fold.
- **Escalation Visibility:**
  - Escalations are always visible but not overwhelming.
- **AI Guidance Clarity:**
  - AI Guidance is clear but does not compete with primary surfaces.
- **Confusion Points:**
  - Some visual competition between Next Best Action and Workflow Chain.
  - Specialist summaries can be verbose, diluting scan clarity.

## 2. Surface Ownership Audit
- **Executive Health Strip:** Purposeful, essential, no overlap.
- **Next Best Action:** Essential, but can compete visually with Workflow Chain.
- **Workforce Room:** Essential for AI context, but can become dense.
- **Workflow Chain:** Essential for orchestration, but must remain compact.
- **Escalation Lane:** Essential, calm, persistent.
- **Executive Snapshot:** Useful, but can be moved lower or collapsed.
- **AI Guidance:** Useful, but should remain secondary.
- **Operational Surfaces:** Some risk of duplication with other panels.
- **Noisy/Overlapping:** Workflow Chain and Next Best Action can compete; Executive Snapshot and Operational Surfaces can overlap.

## 3. Workflow Chain Audit
- **Readability:**
  - Chain is readable and compact, but can become noisy with too many steps.
- **Continuity:**
  - Handoff and blocked states are visible, but escalation overlays can add visual weight.
- **Escalation Clarity:**
  - Escalations are clear, but must remain calm.
- **Pressure Visibility:**
  - Orchestration pressure is visible but subtle.
- **Blocked-State:**
  - Blocked steps are distinct but not alarming.
- **Alive Feel:**
  - Workflow feels more alive than static, but risks noise if steps grow.

## 4. Workforce Room Audit
- **Specialist Readability:**
  - Primary specialist is clear; secondary specialists are compact.
- **Hierarchy:**
  - Hierarchy is preserved, but density can increase quickly.
- **AI Coordination:**
  - Feels coordinated, but summaries can be verbose.
- **Continuity:**
  - Operational continuity is visible.
- **Density:**
  - Risk of overload if more specialists are added.
- **Premium Feel:**
  - Feels premium, but could be more compact.

## 5. Next Best Action Audit
- **Recommendation Clarity:**
  - Clear and actionable, but can compete with workflow chain.
- **Urgency:**
  - Urgency is visible but not alarming.
- **Guidance:**
  - Guidance is operationally useful.
- **Dominance:**
  - Can dominate visually if not kept compact.
- **Actionability:**
  - Users can execute quickly, but scan order must be preserved.

## 6. Cognitive Load Audit
- **Density:**
  - Overall density is rising; risk of overload if more surfaces are added.
- **Spacing:**
  - Generally good, but some surfaces are tight.
- **Scanning Fatigue:**
  - Risk if summaries or chains grow.
- **Information Overload:**
  - Not yet overloaded, but at risk.
- **Motion Risk:**
  - Low (no heavy animation).
- **Surface Competition:**
  - Next Best Action and Workflow Chain compete most.
- **Calmness:**
  - Home remains calm, but at the edge of cognitive load.

## 7. AI Operating System Identity Audit
- **AI-Native Elements:**
  - Workflow Chain, Workforce Room, Escalation Lane, and Orchestration Pressure are strong AI-native elements.
- **Legacy-Dashboard Elements:**
  - Executive Snapshot, some card layouts, and Operational Surfaces feel more dashboard-like.
- **Identity:**
  - Home is shifting toward an AI operating system, but legacy dashboard elements remain.

## 8. Visual System Audit
- **Typography:**
  - Mostly consistent; some card subtitles and summaries are verbose or wrap awkwardly.
- **Spacing:**
  - Generally consistent, but some surfaces are dense.
- **Rhythm:**
  - Visual rhythm is good, but can break with too many cards/steps.
- **Card Weight:**
  - Card weight is balanced, but risk of visual heaviness if more surfaces are added.
- **Calmness/Premium:**
  - Feels premium, but polish can be improved in summaries and spacing.

## 9. Runtime & UX Risk Audit
- **Rerender Risks:** Low (projection-only, no loops).
- **Interaction Fatigue:** Moderate if more surfaces or steps are added.
- **Scaling Risks:** High if more workflow steps or specialists are added without compaction.
- **CSS Growth Risks:** Moderate; must keep selectors isolated and deduplicated.
- **Orchestration Complexity Risks:** Moderate; must avoid BPM/graph complexity.

## 10. Strategic Recommendations
- **Immediate Priorities:**
  - Keep Workflow Chain and Workforce Room compact.
  - Limit specialist summary length.
  - Reduce visual competition between Next Best Action and Workflow Chain.
  - Collapse or move Executive Snapshot lower.
  - Keep Escalation Lane calm and persistent.
- **What NOT to Add:**
  - No BPM diagrams, node graphs, or heavy animation.
  - No additional dashboard widgets.
- **What to Keep Compact:**
  - Workflow Chain, Workforce Room, Escalation Lane.
- **What to Make Dynamic Later:**
  - Workflow handoff transitions, escalation overlays (with restraint).
- **What to Keep Static:**
  - Executive Health Strip, basic AI Guidance.
- **What to Move to Secondary Pages:**
  - Detailed Executive Snapshot, Operational Surfaces.

## 11. Final Verdict
- **Home Maturity:** High — all core surfaces present, but at risk of overload.
- **AI OS Maturity:** Medium-High — strong AI-native elements, but legacy dashboard remains.
- **Executive UX Maturity:** High — scan order and decision clarity are strong.
- **Visual Maturity:** Medium-High — premium feel, but polish and compaction needed.
- **Orchestration Maturity:** Medium — alive, but must avoid noise and complexity.

**Estimate:**
- MH-OS Home is 80% of the way to a world-class AI operating surface. Final 20% requires compaction, polish, and further reduction of legacy dashboard elements.

# HOME PASS 2 STRICT VISUAL REFINEMENT RULESET

## 1. Executive Visual Hierarchy Rules
- **Dominance**: mhos-next-action must be the clear visual focal point; home-exec-header and mhos-executive-strip must support, not compete.
- **Secondary**: home-snapshot-grid, home-workspace-main, and home-ai-guidance-panel must remain visually secondary to executive anchors.
- **No Competition**: No section may visually compete with mhos-next-action or executive anchors.
- **Persistence**: Executive anchors (header, strip, next-action, workflow chain) must remain persistently visible and never hidden or collapsed.

## 2. Cognitive Calmness Rules
- **Spacing Discipline**: Use consistent, generous whitespace between major sections; avoid crowding.
- **Density Discipline**: Limit the number of visible signals per section; avoid overloading any grid or panel.
- **Whitespace Philosophy**: Whitespace is a tool for executive calm, not wasted space.
- **Scanning Reduction**: Layout must guide the eye in a clear, top-down executive flow; avoid zig-zag or scattered scanning paths.
- **Noise Prevention**: Minimize visual clutter, redundant icons, and excessive color/badge use.

## 3. Snapshot Grid Refinement Rules
- **Executive Signals**: Each card must feel like a high-signal executive alert, not a generic metric.
- **Helper Text**: Helper/contextual text should be consolidated at the grid or section level, not repeated per card.
- **Metric-Dashboard Avoidance**: No dense metric tables, no KPI overload, no dashboard sprawl.
- **Repetition Reduction**: Remove or consolidate repetitive elements; use grouping and section headers for context.

## 4. AI Guidance Visual Rules
- **Appearance**: AI guidance must be visually calm, contextual, and clearly supportive—not dominant or overwhelming.
- **Verbosity Control**: Limit visible AI prompts and explanations; use concise, actionable language.
- **Support Role**: AI must reinforce executive action and workflow, not distract or take over the page.
- **Contextuality**: AI guidance should appear only when relevant, and be visually linked to the current executive focus.

## 5. Workflow Chain Visual Rules
- **Operational Movement**: Workflow chain must visually communicate movement and progress, not static personas.
- **Ownership**: Active roles must be clearly highlighted; ownership should be visually distinct but not loud.
- **Handoffs**: Handoffs must be clear, subtle, and never visually jarring.
- **Continuity**: Workflow must remain visible and readable at all times, without adding clutter or fragmentation.

## 6. Governance Visibility Rules
- **Escalation**: Escalation signals must be persistent, visually distinct, and never hidden or minimized.
- **Approval**: Approval status must be clearly visible in the executive strip or relevant panels.
- **Operational Safety**: Safety warnings and operational risks must be visually prioritized when present.
- **Authority**: Authority signals (who is in control, who can approve) must always be visible.
- **Never Hidden**: No governance-critical signal may be hidden, collapsed, or visually deprioritized.

## 7. Forbidden Visual Patterns
- Dashboard sprawl
- Widget competition
- Visual shouting (excessive color, bold, or animation)
- Badge overload
- Metric addiction (too many KPIs/metrics)
- AI clutter (too many prompts, overlays, or explanations)
- Excessive helper text
- Spacing chaos (inconsistent or crowded spacing)
- Over-animation or distracting transitions
- Visual fragmentation (scattered, ungrouped elements)

## 8. Pass 2 Refinement Boundaries
- **May Refine**: Copy, section headers, helper text consolidation, whitespace, grouping, visual calmness, workflow/AI prominence (within hierarchy rules).
- **Must NOT Touch**: Executive anchors, IDs, handlers, data attributes, runtime logic, backend calls, app shell, or any contractually protected surface.
- **Requires Executive Review**: Any change to executive anchors, workflow chain structure, escalation/approval visibility, or persistent governance signals.

## 9. Final MH-OS Home Visual Identity
- The page must feel calm, focused, and authoritative—never busy, crowded, or overwhelming.
- Executive priorities must be instantly clear; the eye should be guided naturally from top (header/strip) to action (next best action) to supporting context (workspace, AI, workflow).
- Visual language must reinforce trust, clarity, and operational safety at all times.

---

This ruleset is non-negotiable for Pass 2. All visual refinements must protect executive cognition, operational calmness, and governance visibility, ensuring MH-OS Home remains a world-class executive operating surface.

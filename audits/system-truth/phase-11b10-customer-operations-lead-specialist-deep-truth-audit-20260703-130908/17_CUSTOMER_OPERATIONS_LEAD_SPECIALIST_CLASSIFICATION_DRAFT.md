# Phase 11B.10 Customer Operations Lead Specialist Classification Draft

## Classification categories

### A - Strong / production-ready
The Customer Operations Lead is safe, wired, data-grounded, useful, and has clear UX.

### B - Strong but needs UX polish
The core role and workflow exist, but the user experience can become more realistic and premium.

### C - Present but shallow
The role exists but outputs are template-heavy or not deeply grounded enough.

### D - Partially wired
Frontend exists, but backend, data, handoff, ticket, inbox, conversation, SLA, CRM, governance, or owning workspace wiring is incomplete.

### E - Missing capability
A required Customer Operations Lead capability is absent.

### F - Safety/compliance blocker
Improvement cannot proceed until authority or governance is clarified.

## Things to classify

- Profile/personality
- Chat behavior
- File/library/source/handoff input
- Inbox review workflow
- Customer thread summary workflow
- Safe reply draft workflow
- Ticket draft workflow
- SLA risk workflow
- Escalation routing workflow
- Customer profile snapshot workflow
- Support / Sales / Operations routing workflow
- Customer Center ownership
- Operations Centers connection
- Task Center handoff connection
- Governance handoff connection
- Sales / CRM handoff connection
- Backend customer operations authority
- Whether AI can send replies or only draft
- Whether AI can create live tickets or only prepare
- Whether AI can change SLA or escalate without confirmation
- Overlap with Operations Lead and Sales / CRM Lead
- Output preview quality
- UI/UX fit for a real customer operations employee
- Tools and quick actions
- Safety boundaries

## Rule
No patch until exact gap and safe implementation plan are proven.

## Strong Areas

### Profile

The Customer Operations Lead is defined as Customer Experience Operations Lead.

It can:
- review inbox context
- summarize customer threads
- draft safe replies
- prepare ticket drafts
- flag SLA and escalation risk

It cannot:
- send customer replies
- create live tickets
- change SLA policy
- escalate without confirmation

Classification:
- Strong.

### Customer Center Connection

Customer Center exists as a protected customer operations surface.

It supports:
- inbox visibility
- conversation previews
- ticket/SLA state
- customer context
- safe AI handoff preparation
- Task Center handoff
- Governance handoff
- AI Command handoff

Classification:
- Strong foundation.

### Backend Customer Operations Authority

Backend routes exist for:
- customer operations readiness
- inbox
- conversations
- conversation detail
- conversation messages
- tickets
- channels
- SLA
- escalations

The backend documents these routes as read-only projections.

They do not:
- send customer replies
- mutate CRM
- create or update tickets
- assign conversations
- place calls
- trigger IVR
- send provider messages
- auto-reply

Classification:
- Strong and safe.

### Safety

Customer Operations Lead output is review / summary / draft / handoff only.

Customer-facing action, CRM mutation, ticket mutation, conversation assignment, SLA change, escalation, phone/IVR/provider execution, and auto-reply are blocked from AI Command and Customer Center in this phase.

Classification:
- Strong.

## Product Gaps

### Gap 1 — Premium Customer Operations Office UX is not final

Current surfaces exist, but the final Customer Operations Lead should eventually feel like a real customer operations office:

- Unified Inbox Board
- Conversation Review Board
- Reply Draft Board
- Ticket Draft Board
- SLA Risk Board
- Escalation Queue
- Customer Profile Snapshot
- Support-to-Sales Handoff Board
- Task Center Handoff Viewer
- Governance Risk Review Panel

Classification:
- Strong but needs UX polish.

### Gap 2 — Live reply sending must remain gated

The system should not send replies from AI Command.

Future reply sending must require:
- selected conversation
- exact drafted reply
- channel confirmation
- identity/permission check
- human approval
- audit log
- provider send result

Classification:
- Correctly blocked now.

### Gap 3 — Live ticket mutation must remain gated

Ticket creation/update should require:
- explicit confirmation
- owner assignment
- status change rules
- SLA impact check
- audit trail
- rollback/reopen behavior

Classification:
- Correctly blocked now.

### Gap 4 — CRM mutation is not final and must remain separated

Customer Operations may prepare CRM handoff context.

Actual CRM update or pipeline mutation should stay with Sales / CRM Lead and owning CRM workflow.

Classification:
- Correctly separated.

### Gap 5 — SLA and escalation execution need dedicated authority audit

SLA risk and escalation context exist.

But live SLA policy changes or escalations must be audited separately before enabling mutation.

Classification:
- Strong foundation, execution not enabled.

### Gap 6 — Overlap with Operations Lead and Sales / CRM Lead is controlled but should be audited globally

Customer Operations Lead owns support/customer communication context.

Operations Lead owns tasks/workflows/execution planning.

Sales / CRM Lead owns lead qualification, outreach drafts, CRM summaries, and sales pipeline handoffs.

This is not harmful duplication, but it should be verified in the final full-team boundary audit.

Classification:
- Healthy overlap.

### Gap 7 — Profile/Avatar/Voice customization is missing as a durable specialist system

Customer Operations Lead has static role/profile definition, but no proven final user-configurable profile system for:
- display name
- avatar/profile photo
- male/female/neutral voice
- voice provider
- support tone
- escalation strictness
- default reply style
- SLA urgency preference

Classification:
- Missing capability.

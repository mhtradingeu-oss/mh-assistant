# Phase 11B.9 Operations Lead Specialist Classification Draft

## Classification categories

### A - Strong / production-ready
The Operations Lead is safe, wired, data-grounded, useful, and has clear UX.

### B - Strong but needs UX polish
The core role and workflow exist, but the user experience can become more realistic and premium.

### C - Present but shallow
The role exists but outputs are template-heavy or not deeply grounded enough.

### D - Partially wired
Frontend exists, but backend, data, handoff, workflow, task, queue, notification, approval, or owning workspace wiring is incomplete.

### E - Missing capability
A required Operations Lead capability is absent.

### F - Safety/compliance blocker
Improvement cannot proceed until authority or governance is clarified.

## Things to classify

- Profile/personality
- Chat behavior
- File/library/source/handoff input
- Task planning workflow
- Workflow draft workflow
- Handoff summary workflow
- Execution checklist workflow
- Timeline/owner/dependency workflow
- Workflows page ownership
- Task Center connection
- Queue Center connection
- Job Monitor connection
- Notifications connection
- Backend operations / workflow / task / handoff authority
- Governance / approval gates
- Whether AI can execute workflows or only prepare
- Whether AI can auto-approve or override authority
- Overlap with Publisher / Compliance / Customer Ops / Sales CRM
- Output preview quality
- UI/UX fit for a real operations employee
- Tools and quick actions
- Safety boundaries

## Rule
No patch until exact gap and safe implementation plan are proven.
